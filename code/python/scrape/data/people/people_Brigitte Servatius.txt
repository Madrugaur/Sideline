[4] She moved to the US in 1981, to begin doctoral studies at Syracuse University.[2] She completed her Ph.D. in 1987,[5] and joined the Worcester Polytechnic Institute [4]  faculty in the same Servatius) of the book Combinatorial Rigidity (1993).[6][G]\nAnother well-cited paper of hers in this area characterizes the planar Laman graphs, the minimally rigid graphs that can be embedded
