{"name": "Bonita V. Saunders", "content": "Bonita V. Saunders is an American mathematician specializing in mathematical visualization. She works at the National Institute of Standards and Technology in the Applied and Computational Mathematics Division of the Information Technology Laboratory, where she contributes to the Digital Library of Mathematical Functions as the Visualization Editor and the principal designer of visualizations and graphs.[1]\n Saunders received her PhD in computational and applied mathematics from Old Dominion University in 1985.[1] She earned her M.S. in Mathematics from the University of Virginia and her B.A. in Mathematics from the College of William and Mary.[2]  She worked at the BDM corporation for four years and has published a number of papers.[2] A list of her papers can be found at [1].\n Saunders has made a number of contributions to the Mathematical Association of America.  From 1999-2002, she served as the MD-DC-VA Section treasurer.[3]  From 2004-2009, she was on the Selection Committee for the Etta Z. Falconer Lecture (sponsored by MAA and AWM).[3]  She was also on the MAA Business Industry Government Committee from 2012-2017,[3] and was the MD-DC-VA Section Representative to the MAA Congress in 2019.[3]  As of 2020 she is on the editorial board for Mathematics Magazine.[3]\n The National Association of Mathematicians chose Saunders to give the 2001 Claytor Lecture at the Joint Mathematics Meetings.[1]\n The US Department of Commerce awarded her research group a Gold Medal for Distinguished Scientific/Engineering Achievement in 2011, in recognition for their work in creating the online Digital Library of Mathematical Functions.[4]\n In 2018, Mathematically Gifted & Black made Saunders a Black History Month honoree.[5]\n In 2019 she accepted the Excellence in Research in Mathematics and Computer Science award from the Washington Academy of Sciences[6] and became a Fellow of the Washington Academy of Sciences.[1]\n ", "tags": ["Living people", "20th-century American mathematicians", "21st-century American mathematicians", "American women mathematicians", "African-American mathematicians", "Old Dominion University alumni", "University of Virginia alumni", "College of William & Mary alumni", "Articles with short description", "Short description matches Wikidata", "Articles with hCards", "Wikipedia articles with ORCID identifiers", "Wikipedia articles with VIAF identifiers", "Wikipedia articles with WorldCat-VIAF identifiers", "Year of birth missing (living people)"], "raw": "American mathematician\nBonita V.
