He is known, along with C. S. Seshadri, for their proof of the Narasimhan\u2013Seshadri theorem, and both were elected as Fellows of the Royal Society. Fr Racine had studied with the famous French mathematicians \u00c9lie Cartan and Jacques Hadamard, and connected his students with the latest developments in modern mathematics. Among Racine's other students who achieved eminence are Subbaramiah Minakshisundaram, K. G. Ramanathan, C S Seshadri, Raghavan Narasimhan, and C. P. Ramanujam.[citation needed]\nNarasimhan went to the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR), Bombay, for his graduate studies. He obtained his Ph.D. from University of Mumbai in 1960; his advisor was K. S.
