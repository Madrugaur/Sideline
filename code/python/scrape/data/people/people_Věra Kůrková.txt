Her research interests include neural networks, computational learning theory, and nonlinear approximation theory.[2]  She formulated the abstract concept of a variational norm in\n1997 which puts ideas of Maurey, Jones, and Barron into the context of functional analysis. See \nV. K\u016frkov\u00e1, Dimension-independent rates of approximation by neural networks.\nIn: Warwick, K., Karny, M. (eds.) Computer-Intensive Methods in Control\nand Signal Processing. Anzellotti, Convergence rates of approximation by translates, MIT Artificial Intelligence Laboratory, AI Memo No. 1288, April 1995, C.B.I.P. Paper No. 73.  K\u016frkov\u00e1 is also known for the concept of quasiorthogonal set which she developed jointly with Robert Hecht-Nielsen and Paul Kainen.\n K\u016frkov\u00e1 earned a Ph.D. in 1980 and a habilitation in 1999, both from Charles University. She has been affiliated with the Czech Academy of Sciences since 1990, and she headed the Department of Theoretical Computer Science within the Institute of Computer Science from 2002 to 2008.[3]\n In 2010, the Czech Academy of Sciences awarded K\u016frkov\u00e1 the Bernard Bolzano Honorary Medal for Merit in the Mathematical Sciences.[3][4]\nAs of 2018[update], K\u016frkov\u00e1 is president of the European Neural Network Society.[5]  For recent work, see V.
