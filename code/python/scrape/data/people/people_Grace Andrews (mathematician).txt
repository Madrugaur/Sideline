Her dissertation was The Primitive Double Minimal Surface of the Seventh Class and its Conjugate.[3]\n She worked as an Assistant Professor of Mathematics for Barnard College from 1900 to 1902. She then served as accountant to the Treasurer for Wesleyan University from 1903 to 1926, working from her home in Brooklyn.
