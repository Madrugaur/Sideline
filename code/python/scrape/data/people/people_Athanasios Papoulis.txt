He earned his undergraduate degree from National Technical University of Athens. In 1945, he stowed away on a boat to escape the impending Greek Civil War and settled in the United States. [One was] Athanasios Papoulis' Probability, Random Variables, and Stochastic Processes... These books popularized a pedagogy that balanced rigor and intuition.[5]\nBy staying away from complete mathematical rigor while emphasizing the physical and engineering interpretations of probability, Papoulis's book gained wide popularity.\n\nTheory[edit]\nAthanasios Papoulis specialized in engineering mathematics, his work covers probability, statistics, and estimation in the application of these fields to modern engineering problems. Papoulis also taught and developed subjects such as stochastic simulation, mean square estimation, likelihood tests, maximum entropy methods, Monte Carlo method, spectral representations and estimation, sampling theory, bispectrum and system identification, cyclostationary processes, deterministic signals in noise (part of deterministic systems and dynamical system studies), wave optics and the Wiener and Kalman filters.\n\nContributions[edit]\nPapoulis's generalization of the sampling theorem[6] unified many variations of the Nyquist\u2013Shannon sampling theorem into one theorem.[7][8]\nThe Papoulis\u2013Gerchberg algorithm[9][10][11] is an iterative signal restoration algorithm that has found widespread use in signal and image processing.[12][13]\n\"Papoulis's eloquent proof\"[14] of the conventional sampling theorem[15] requires only two equations.\nBibliography[edit]\nThe Fourier Integral and its Applications by Papoulis, Athanasios, McGraw-Hill Companies (June 1, 1962), ISBN\u00a00-07-048447-3, LCCN\u00a062--10211.\nProbability, Random Variables, and Stochastic Processes by Papoulis, Athanasios 1965.
