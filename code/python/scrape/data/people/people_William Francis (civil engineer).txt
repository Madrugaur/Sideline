{"name": "William Francis (civil engineer)", "content": "\n Sir Horace William Alexander Francis CBE FREng[1] (born 31 August 1926) is a British civil engineer.\n Francis was born in Clydebank, Scotland on 31 August 1926, son of Horace Fairie Francis and his wife Jane McMinn Murray[2] and studied at Glasgow's Royal Technical College, which is now part of Strathclyde University.[3][4][2]\n He married in 1949 Gwendoline Maud Dorricott and has two sons and two daughters.[2]\n He has worked on many construction projects in the United Kingdom and abroad including manufacturing facilities, bridges, power stations and offshore structures.[5] He spent 25 years working with the engineering contractor Tarmac plc and was the company's chief operating officer and vice-chairman.[5] Francis has also served as executive director of construction for the Trafalgar House conglomerate and as non-executive director of its oil and gas interests.[5] He has worked as a government advisor for approximately 30 years, serving on several advisory boards such as the British Overseas Trade Board and the Export Credit Guarantee Department.[5] Francis has also worked as a director of the British Railways Board between 1994 and 1997 and as chairman of the Black Country Development Corporation.[2][5]\n On 1 November 1982 Francis was appointed major in the British Army's Engineer and Railway Staff Corps, an invitation-only, unpaid unit of 60 engineering and logistics professionals that provide advice to the British armed forces on specialist and technical matters.[6][7][8] He was promoted to lieutenant-colonel on 8 July 1986 and became supernumerary to the unit on 17 August 1992.[9][10]\n Francis is now semi-retired and works as director of Peakbeam Ltd and Longden Properties.[11] He is a principal partner in Security Composites Limited, a thermoplastic manufacturer based in Shrewsbury, where he has developed a thermoplastic-based formwork for in-situ concrete works.[12] Francis is an Honorary Life Vice-President of the Lighthouse Club, a national charity that provides for the families of construction workers killed or injured at work.  He has been a member for over 40 years and served as president from 1990\u20135 and helped the club to introduce a corporate membership grade.[13]\n Francis was appointed a Commander of the Order of November 1988.[11][13][15] He became a knight bachelor on 25 July 1989 in a ceremony at Buckingham Palace.[16] Francis is involved with the Civil Engineers Club, a social organisation for members of
