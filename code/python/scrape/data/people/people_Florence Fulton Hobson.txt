She passed her preliminary examination with the Royal Institute of British Architects (RBA) in 1899. Hobson moved to London, where she worked in the office of Guy Dawber and then with James Glen Sivewright Gibson from 1903 to 1904. Hobson then returned to Ireland and became part of the architectural staff of Belfast Corporation in 1905. She was elected a licentiate of the RBA in 1911. While working as an assistant to the Royal Commission on Health and Housing, she travelled to Germany and Switzerland to study housing issues in those countries. She later worked on the Reconstruction Commission of the Irish White Cross. Hobson retired in 1937.[1][2][3]\n In 1948, she married William Forbes Patterson. The couple first lived in Crawfordsburn but is known to have been living in London during the period from 1957 to 1965.
