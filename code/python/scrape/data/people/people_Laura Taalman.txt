Her mathematical research concerns knot theory and singular algebraic geometry; she is a professor of mathematics at James Madison University.\n Taalman earned a bachelor's degree in mathematics from the University of Chicago in 1994.[1] She completed her Ph.D. at Duke University in 2000; her dissertation, Monomial Generators for the Nash Sheaf of a Complete Resolution, was supervised by William L. Her models have included subjects from mathematics including knots, fractals, and snap-together polyhedra.[3][4]\n With Peter Kohn, Taalman is the author of a textbook Calculus.[5] She is also the author of a different text, Calculus I with Integrated Precalculus.\n With Jason Rosenhouse (also a mathematics professor at James Madison University) she is the author of Taking Sudoku Seriously: The Mathematics Behind the World\u2019s Most Popular Pencil Puzzle.[6] She has also written a series of Sudoku puzzle books with Philip Riley.[7]\n Taalman won the Trevor Evans Award of the Mathematical Association of America in 2003 for her work with Eug\u00e9nie Hunsicker on the mathematics of Modular architecture.[8]\n In 2005, Taalman won the Henry L.
