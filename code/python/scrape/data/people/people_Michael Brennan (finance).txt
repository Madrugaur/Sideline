Brennan (born November 14, 1942) is emeritus professor of finance at the UCLA Anderson School of Management.[1] Brennan co-designed the Brennan-Schwartz interest rate model[2] and was a pioneer of real options theory.[3] His writings on real options and asset pricing, corporate finance, derivative securities, market microstructure, the role of information in capital markets, and risk management have been published extensively.\n He is a former president of the American Finance Association, and has served as editor of the Journal of Finance and was the founding editor of the Review of Financial Studies; the Michael Brennan Award is named for him. He was a founding partner and director of the Real Options Group since its inception. He has served as consultant to businesses and governments in the USA and Canada. He was previously a professor at the London Business School.\n Professor Brennan holds a B.Phil. in Economics (1964) from Oxford University, an MBA (1967) from the University of Pittsburgh and a Ph.D. in Business (1970) from the MIT Sloan School of Management.\n On May 22, 2011, Brennan was awarded an honorary Doctor of Laws from the University of Notre Dame.[4]\n \n \n This biography of an American economist is a stub.
