He also developed and constructed a large gasoline engine for his dirigible.[1] In 1879 he demonstrated his flying models of a helicopter, aircraft and ornithopter, while in 1881 approached the building of an aircraft.[citation needed] In the catalog of aeronautical exhibition 1911 and in the article of G.V. Ewald's report in the Russian technical society on 12 March 1883.[citation needed]\n Ognjeslav lived with his family in Saint Petersburg, Russia.
