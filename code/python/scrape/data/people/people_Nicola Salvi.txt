His mentor in architecture was Antonio Canevari, who served also as consulting architect of the king of Portugal. In 1728, Canevari left for Lisbon, and Salvi continued his commissions in Rome. The former completion was won by Alessandro Galilei, though Salvi\u2019s design had much praise. Salvi\u2019s design for the fountain however was chosen instead of plans by Ferdinando Fuga and his friend Luigi Vanvitelli.
