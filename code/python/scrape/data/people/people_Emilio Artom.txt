In 1914 he married Amalia Artom (also a  mathematician) and they had two children: historian and partisan Emanuele (1915\u20131944) and Ennio (1920\u20131940), who died doing mountain trekking. Artom hence committed himself to linguistic studies.\n The tragic events of the family and the entire Italian Jewish community after the 1938 Italian Racial Laws kept him permanently away from the study of mathematics.
