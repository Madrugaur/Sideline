(Marty) Demaine (born 1942[1]) is an artist and mathematician, the Angelika and Barton Weller artist in residence at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.[2]\n Demaine attended Medford High School in Medford, Massachusetts.[3] After studying glassblowing in England, he began his artistic career by blowing art glass in New Brunswick in the early 1970s.[4] The Demaine Studio, located in Miramichi Bay and later at Opus Village in Mactaquac, was the first one-man glass studio in Canada,[5] part of the international studio glass movement. Demaine's pieces from this period are represented in the permanent collections of half a dozen major museums[6] including the Canadian Museum of Civilization[7] and the National Gallery of Canada. Since joining MIT, Demaine has begun blowing glass again, as an instructor at the MIT Glass Lab;[8] his newer work features innovative glassblowing techniques intended as a puzzle to his fellow glassblowers.[4][9]\n Martin Demaine is the father of MIT Computer Science professor and MacArthur Fellow Erik Demaine;
