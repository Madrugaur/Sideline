His final and most recognized work is the Hallgr\u00edmskirkja church, which was commissioned in 1937.\n Not much of his early life is known. Gu\u00f0j\u00f3n's design of Hallgr\u00edmskirkja was, for instance, inspired by basalt columns, such as those at Svartifoss.[5] Gu\u00f0j\u00f3n characterized his design of the main-building of the University of Iceland as a distinct and new Icelandic style that he believed would contribute to the revival of the Icelandic Commonwealth.
