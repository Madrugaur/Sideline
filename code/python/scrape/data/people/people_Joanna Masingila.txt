{"name": "Joanna Masingila", "content": "\nJoanna Osborne Masingila (born 1960)[1] is an American mathematics educator. She is dean of the School of Education at Syracuse University, Laura J. and L. Douglas Meredith Professor for Teaching Excellence, and a professor of mathematics and mathematics education at Syracuse.[2]\n Masingila is originally from Oregon, and moved to Kansas as a child. She was a student at Hesston College,[3] and graduated from Goshen College in 1982, with a bachelor's degree in mathematics. After earning a master's degree in mathematics education from Indiana University\u2013Purdue University Indianapolis in 1987, she completed a Ph.D. in mathematics education at Indiana University Bloomington in 1992.[2] Her dissertation, Mathematics Practice and Apprenticeship in Carpet Laying: Suggestions for Mathematics Education, was supervised by Frank K. Lester Jr.[4]\n While completing her graduate education, Masingila worked for six years as a secondary-school mathematics teacher. She joined the Syracuse University faculty in 1992, and was named Laura J. and L. Douglas Meredith Professor for Teaching Excellence in 2003.[2] After becoming interim dean of education in 2014, she was named dean in 2015.[5]\n Masingila's husband, Adamson (1956-2021), was from Kenya and also studied at Hesston College and Goshen College.
