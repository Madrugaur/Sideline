Morgilevsky Architectural and Town-planning Prize in 1999; Honoured Cultural Worker of Ukraine, 1995\nVictor Vechersky (born 15 August 1958 Kyiv, in the Ukrainian SSR of the Soviet Union, in present-day Ukraine) is a Ukrainian architect. He graduated from the Kyiv State Institute of Art in 1981, architectural department. Ph.D. (in architecture) since 2001.\nAwards, prizes, honorary titles: State Prize in the field of Architecture in 1998 and 2007; I. Morgilevsky Architectural and Town-planning Prize in 1999; Honoured Cultural Worker of Ukraine, 1995.\nCurrent occupation: an architect, a scientist (Deputy Head of the Institute of Monumemt Protection Research under the Ministry of Culture and Tourism of Ukraine), a teacher (Asst. Prof. National Academy of Visual Arts and Architecture).\nVictor Vechersky is the author of numerous architectural studies, including:\nResearches and Restoration:\n\nMovchansky Monastery in Putyvl (1981);\nWooden Fortress in Putyvl (1986);\nReconstruction of the House of Ukrainian Painter Mykola Pymonenko in the village of Maliutianka (1990).\nHistorical architectural plans and the projects of protection areas for historical cities of Ukraine:\n\nHlukhiv (1984);\nPoltava (1984);\nLebedyn (1985);\nChernivtsi (1986, 2006);\nChyhyryn (1987);\nPutyvl (1987);\nRomny (1987);\nOkhtyrka (1988);\nTrostianets (1989);\nBilopillia (1990);\nKonotop (1991);\nSumy (1993, 2011);\nOdessa (2007);\nVasylkiv (2008);\nKyiv (2009\u20132010).\nProjects of creation state historical-architectural preserves in Putyvl (1986), Hlukhiv (1992), Sumy (1993). Rehabilitation and preservation of historical town-building formation in Hlukhiv (1997). State program of preservation of historical town-building formation in Hlukhiv (1999).
