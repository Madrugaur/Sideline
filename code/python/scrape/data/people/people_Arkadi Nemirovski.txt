He has won three prestigious prizes: the Fulkerson Prize, the George B. Dantzig Prize, and the John von Neumann Theory Prize.[5]\nHe was elected to the U.S. National Academy of Engineering (NAE) in 2017 \"for the development of efficient algorithms for large-scale convex optimization problems\",[6] and the U.S National Academy of Sciences (NAS) in 2020.[7]\n\nAcademic work[edit]\nHis work with Yurii Nesterov in their 1994 book[8] is the first to point out that the interior point method can solve convex optimization problems, and the first to make a systematic study of semidefinite programming (SDP).
