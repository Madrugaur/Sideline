{"name": "Teodoro Gonz\u00e1lez de Le\u00f3n", "content": "\n Teodoro Gonz\u00e1lez de Le\u00f3n (May 29, 1926 \u2013 September 16, 2016) was a Mexican architect.[1][2]\n Gonz\u00e1lez de Le\u00f3n studied at the Escuela Nacional de Arquitectura of the Universidad Nacional Aut\u00f3noma de M\u00e9xico (UNAM) from 1942 to 1947. Thanks to a scholarship from the French government, he worked in France for 18 months with Le Corbusier, from 1948 to 1949. During this time, he was involved mainly on the Unit\u00e9 d'Habitation project by Le Corbusier in Marseille and on the St Di\u00e9 factory project.[3]\n Still as student, he conceived the original concept and idea for the campus \"Ciudad Universitaria\" of the U.N.A.M., the main university in Mexico and that essence was developed by his own teachers and the great architects of the time. The complex is part of the World Heritage as named by the UNESCO where several famous muralists - Diego Rivera, Juan O'Gorman - made important works. Between 1955 and 1965 he planned an urban planning series and made several construction plannings. \n After 1969, his work was influenced by Abraham Zabludovsky. Together with him, he designed several public buildings between 1974 and 1982, amongst them the Delegaci\u00f3n Cuauht\u00e9moc, the COLMEX building, the seat of the Instituto del Fondo Nacional de la Vivienda para los Trabajadores (INFONAVIT), the Universidad Pedag\u00f3gica Nacional and the Museo Rufino Tamayo. From 1984 to 1987 he designed several distinctive buildings in Villahermosa, amongst them in cooperation with J. Francisco Serrano Cacho the administration center. Together with Serrano Cacho, he also designed the Mexican embassy in Berlin-Tiergarten (2000\u20132001).[4] He also designed the Palacio de Justicia Federal in Mexico City and the mixed-use development Reforma 222 containing two of the 30 tallest buildings in Mexico City,[5] and the Torre Manacar in southern Mexico City.[6]\n Gonz\u00e1lez de Le\u00f3n was a numbered member of the Academia de Artes from 1984 on, and beginning in 1988 was member of the Colegio de M\u00e9xico.[7] He was an honorary member of the International Academy of Architecture (IAA)[8] and an emeritus academic of the Academia Nacional de Arquitectura.
