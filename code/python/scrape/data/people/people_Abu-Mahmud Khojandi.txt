He noted that measurements by earlier astronomers had found higher values (Indians: 24\u00b0; Ptolemy 23\u00b0 51') and thus discovered that the axial tilt is not constant but is in fact (currently) decreasing. His measurement of the axial tilt was however about 2 minutes too small, probably due to his heavy instrument settling over the course of the observations.[2][3]\n\nMathematics[edit]\nKhojandi stated a special case of Fermat's last theorem for n = 3, but his attempted proof of the theorem was incorrect.
