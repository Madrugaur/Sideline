Pedersen (Sep 17, 1934\u2013Jan 1, 2016)[1][2] was an American mathematician and author particularly known for her works on the mathematics of paper folding.\n\nEducation and career[edit]\nPedersen was born in Salt Lake City, Utah, the daughter of an ophthalmologist and a teacher. She studied home economics changing to a double major in mathematics and physics as an undergraduate at Brigham Young University, before becoming a graduate student in mathematics at the University of Utah under the supervision of E. Allen Davis.[2]\nAfter completing her master's degree, she moved to San Jose, California, following her husband who worked for IBM. She joined the faculty at the Santa Clara University on a part-time basis in 1966, but shifted to full-time and was promoted to full professor in 1996. She was the first woman to teach mathematics at the university, and the first to be tenured as a mathematics professor.[2]\nHer discovery that the platonic solids could be braided from strips of paper led to Martin Gardner writing about it in the  September, 1971 Mathematical Games column in Scientific American.[3]\n\nBooks[edit]\nPedersen's books include:\n\nGeometric Playthings (With Kent Pedersen, Dale Seymour Publications Secondary, 1973, ISBN\u00a0978-0866513517)\nFear No More: An Adult Approach to Mathematics (with Peter Hilton, Dale Seymour Publications, 1982 ISBN\u00a0978-0201057133)\nBuild Your Own Polyhedra (with Peter Hilton, Addison-Wesley, 1988)[4]\nMathematical Reflections: In a Room with Many Windows (with Peter Hilton and Derek Holton, Springer, 1996)[5][6]\nMathematical Vistas: From a Room with Many Windows (with Peter Hilton and Derek Holton, Springerl 2002)[5][7]\n99 Points of Intersection: Examples\u2014Pictures\u2014Proofs (by Hans Walser, translated with Peter Hilton, Mathematical Association of America, 2006)[8]\nA Mathematical Tapestry: Demonstrating the Beautiful Unity of Mathematics (with Peter Hilton, illustrated by Sylvie Donmoyer, Cambridge University Press, 2010)[9]\nShe and Peter Hilton also translated The Golden Section and Symmetry by Hans Walser from German into English.
