{"name": "Leonard Bocour", "content": "Leonard Bocour (March 18, 1910 \u2013 September 6, 1993) was an American artist.\n Bocour was born in New York City.  Around 1933 he formed the New York City based company Bocour Artists Colors. He was the co-developer along with Sam Golden of Magna paint in the late 1940s. From 1952 until 1970 he and Sam Golden were partners in the Bocour Artists Colors Co. The company sold artist paints from the late 1930s until the 1990s. Many well-known artists from Mark Rothko, Jackson Pollock and Willem de Kooning  to Helen Frankenthaler, Morris Louis, Kenneth Noland and hundreds of others bought paint from Leonard Bocour. The tubes of paint marked Bocour were watercolor or oil paint and the tubes labeled Bellini were oil paint. The acrylic paint was thick bodied and called Aquatec.\n He was a serious art collector, in addition to being a well-known paint manufacturer. He collected and owned works by many of his now famous customers.[1] Bocour donated part of his collection to St. Mary's College of Maryland.[2]\n \n This article about an American businessperson born in the 1910s is a stub.
