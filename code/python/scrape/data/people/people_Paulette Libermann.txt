She began her studies in 1938 at the \u00c9cole normale sup\u00e9rieure de jeunes filles, a college in S\u00e8vres for training women to become teachers, but was prevented from taking the agr\u00e9gation and becoming a teacher because of anti-Jewish laws instituted by the German occupation. After the liberation of Paris in 1944, she returned to S\u00e8vres and completed her studies.[1][2][3]\n She taught briefly in Douai, and then after two years of study with J. H. C. Whitehead at Oxford University she returned to a teaching position in Strasbourg. She obtained a faculty position at the University of Rennes, and moved to the University of Paris in 1966.
