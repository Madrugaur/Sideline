{"name": "Graham Turbott", "content": "\n Evan Graham Turbott QSO (27 May 1914 \u2013 12 December 2014), generally known as Graham Turbott, was a New Zealand ornithologist, zoologist, and museum administrator. He served as director of the Auckland Institute and Museum from 1964 to 1979.\n Born at Stanley Bay on Auckland's North Shore, Turbott was the eldest of the three sons of Thomas Turbott, a primary school headmaster, and his wife Evangeline Alice Turbott (n\u00e9e Graham).[1] His brothers included the diplomat and businessman Ian Turbott. He was educated at Takapuna Grammar School, and went on to study at Auckland Teachers' Training College and Auckland University College.[1] He graduated from the latter institution with a Master of Science in zoology in 1938.[1][2]  His thesis was entitled Some observations on the distribution and anatomy of Leiopelma hochstetteri Fitzinger.\n On 7 September 1940, Turbott married the ethnologist Olwyn Mary Rutherford at All Saints' Church, Howick.[3]\n In 1937 Turbott was appointed as assistant zoologist at Auckland War Memorial Museum.[4] In 1944 he spent a year on coast-watching duties in the subantarctic Auckland Islands as part of the Cape Expedition, which also included Charles Fleming and Robert Falla, but he also found time to pursue his interest in natural history.[1] He later wrote about the experience in the book Year Away: Wartime coast watching on the Auckland Islands, 1944, published in 2002.[5]\n Turbott returned and continued at Auckland Museum, until becoming assistant director of Canterbury Museum, Christchurch in 1957. He returned in 1964 to take up the post of director of the Auckland Institute and Museum, succeeding Sir Gilbert Archey. He remained there until he retired in 1979,[1] when he was named as the museum's director emeritus.[6]\n Turbott wrote or co-authored several ornithological works, including New Zealand bird life (1947), Buller's birds of New Zealand (2nd updated edition, 1967, as editor), A field guide to the birds of New Zealand and outlying Islands (1970, with Robert Falla), The new guide to the birds of birds of New Zealand and the Ross Dependency, Antarctica (1990).\n A founding member of the Ornithological Society of New Zealand, Turbott served as its president from 1949 to 1952, North Island vice president from 1947 to 1949 and 1957 to 1958, and a councillor between 1953 and 1955.[1]\n Turbott died on 12 December 2014.[1]\n In the 1978 New Year Honours, Turbott was appointed a Companion of the Society of New Zealand in 1988, and made a fellow of the same organisation in 1997.[1] In 2014, Turbott was the recipient of a lifetime achievement award from Auckland Museum.[6]\n Almost 20 species,
