In particular, she has applied her research results in automorphic forms and number theory to construct efficient communication networks called Ramanujan graphs and Ramanujan complexes.\n Li did her the National Center of Theoretical Sciences in Taiwan from 2009 to 2014.[1][2]\n In 2010, Li was the winner of the Chern Prize, given every three years to an outstanding Chinese mathematician.[5] In Pennsylvania State University.[1] She is a number theorist, with research focusing on the theory of automorphic forms and applications of number theory to coding theory and spectral graph theory.
