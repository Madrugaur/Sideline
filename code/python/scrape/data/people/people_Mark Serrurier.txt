Mark was a graduate of Caltech and went on to work on designs for the Mt. Palomar 200\u00a0inch (5 m) Hale telescope. The pioneering truss design he invented for that instrument's massive tube structure (the \"Serrurier truss\") is still used today in large telescope designs. During World War II, Mark worked at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory testing jet aircraft engines. In 1942, Rolf Sabersky worked in mechanical design on the Southern California Cooperative Wind Tunnel under Serrurier and Hap Richards.[1][2][3]\nAfter the war in 1946 Mark took over as president of Moviola Co., his father Iwan\u2019s company. Mark re-designed his fathers invention with many improvements and ran the company until he sold it in 1966. In 1979, Mark accepted a special Academy Award for Technical Achievement\" For the progressive development of the Moviola from the 1924 invention of his father, Iwan Serrurier, to the present Series 20 sophisticated film editing equipment\". He only accepted after insisting that his late father's name would also appear on the statue.
