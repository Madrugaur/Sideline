Indeed, when he was afterwards himself tried for his life on the charge of having been concerned in setting up this very government, when the Four Hundred were overthrown and hardly dealt with by the commons, he made what would seem to be the best defence of any known up to my time. Antiphon may be regarded as the founder of political oratory, but he never addressed the people himself except on the occasion of his trial.
