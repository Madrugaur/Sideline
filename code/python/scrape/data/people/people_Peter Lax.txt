He attended a complex analysis class in the role of a student, but ended up taking over as instructor. He was then sent to Oak Ridge National Laboratory, and soon afterwards to the Manhattan Project at Los Alamos, New Mexico. He stayed at NYU for his graduate studies, marrying Anneli in 1948 and earning a PhD in 1949 under the supervision of Kurt O. Friedrichs.[3][4]\n Lax holds a faculty position in the Department of Mathematics, Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences, New York University.[citation needed]\n He is a member of the Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters[5] and the National Academy of Sciences, USA. He stayed at NYU for his graduate studies, marrying Anneli in 1948 and earning a PhD in 1949 under the supervision of Kurt O.
