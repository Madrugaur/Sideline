Koiter[1]Doctoral studentsOnno Boxma\n\nJacob Willem \"Wim\" Cohen (27 August 1923 Leeuwarden \u2013 12 November 2000) was a Dutch mathematician, well known for over hundred scientific publications and several books in queueing theory.\nCohen was born in a Jewish family, as the son of Benjamin Cohen and Aaltje Klein.[2] Having acquired an autodidact knowledge of mathematics while in hiding during World War II, Cohen got an Engineer's degree (1949) and Ph.D. degree (1955) in mechanical engineering at Delft University, on a dissertation entitled Stress Calculations in Helicoidal Shells and Propeller Blades. He worked as teletraffic engineer with the Telecommunications group at  Philips (1950\u201357), at the applied mathematics department at Delft (1957\u201373) and  University of Utrecht (1973-1998).  He was buried in Haifa.[3][4]\n\nBooks[edit]\nThe single server queue (1969)\nStatistical Power Analysis for the Behavioural Sciences (Academic Press, 1969)\nOn regenerative processes in queueing theory\nBoundary value problems in queueing systems (Elsevier, 1983).
