She then attended nearby Bates College from which she graduated in 1922 with honors in both mathematics and science.  Despite her science-focused majors, she also was drawn to and pursued both poetry and music.[2]\n O'Brien continued to take courses in mathematics, at Smith College, from 1922 to 1923. her dissertation was Some Problems in Interpolation by Characteristic Functions of Linear Differential Systems of the Fourth Order.[5]\n In 1940, O'Brien joined the faculty at Deering High School in Portland, Maine, and she taught there until her retirement in 1971.[2]\n O'Brien was the author of Sequences (Houghton-Mifflin Mathematics Enrichment Series, 1966).\nSome of her poetry was collected in the book Excavation and Other Verse (Anthoensen Press, 1967).[6]\n The University of Maine gave O'Brien an honorary doctorate in 1960,[3] and Bowdoin College gave her another in 1965.[3][4]\n O'Brien won the Deborah Morton Award, an annual award for Maine women of distinction, in 1985.[3][7]\n She also became a fellow of the International Academy of Poetry and, in 1967, an elected member of the New York Academy of Sciences.[3]\n ", "tags": ["1901 births", "1986 deaths", "20th-century American mathematicians", "American women mathematicians", "Smith College alumni", "Cornell University alumni", "Brown University alumni", "College of New Rochelle faculty", "People from Amesbury, Massachusetts", "Articles with short description", "Short description matches Wikidata", "Wikipedia articles with MGP identifiers"], "raw": "American mathematician, musician and poet\nKatharine Elizabeth O'Brien (1901 \u2013 1986) was an American mathematician, musician and poet.\n\nContents\n\n1 Early life\n2 Higher education and career\n3 Books\n4 Recognition\n5 References\n6 External links\n\n\nEarly life[edit]\nBorn in Amesbury, Massachusetts to parents who had emigrated from Ireland, O'Brien's family moved to Maine while at the age of three.[1]  She was class valedictorian when she graduated from Deering High School in Portland, Maine in 1917.
