After studying at the Lyc\u00e9e Janson-de-Sailly, she began studies at the \u00c9cole Normale Sup\u00e9rieure in 1934, but contracted tuberculosis which forced her to drop out. She married another Jewish mathematician, Laurent Schwartz, in 1938, and soon went into hiding while the Nazis occupied France. After the war, she taught at the University of Reims Champagne-Ardenne and finished a thesis on generalizations of the Gauss\u2013Bonnet formula in 1953. In 1964, she moved to the University of Lille, where she retired in 1981.[1][3]\n A conference was held in her honor in Lille in 1986, and a day of lectures in Paris honored her 80th birthday in 1993, during which she presented a two-hour talk herself.
