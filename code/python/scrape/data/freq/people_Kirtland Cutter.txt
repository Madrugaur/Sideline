He was born in East Rockport, Ohio, the great-grandson of Jared Potter Kirtland. He studied painting and illustration at the Art Students League of New York. At the age of 26 he moved to Spokane, Washington, and began working as a banker for his uncle. It was a popular favorite, visited by an estimated 18\u00a0million people. The building's design and interior furnishings were a major precursor of the Arts and Crafts movement.\n Cutter also worked in partnership with Karl G.
