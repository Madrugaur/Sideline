{"name": "Dominique Gauzin-M\u00fcller", "content": "Dominique Gauzin-M\u00fcller (born 1960) is a French architect and architectural critic, focusing on wood and sustainability in architecture and urbanism. She is the author of several books on these subjects, which have been translated into several languages. She wrote Construire avec le Bois (1999), L'architecture \u00e9cologique (2001), 25 maisons en bois (2003) and 25 maisons \u00e9cologique (2005). \n Dominique Gauzin-M\u00fcller was born in Vincennes, near Paris, but grew up in Saint-C\u00e9r\u00e9, Lot . She studied architecture at the \u00c9cole d'architecture Paris-Tolbiac, under Roland Schweizer, the French specialist in wooden buildings, and Marion Tournon-Branly. 1984 she received a \"Dipl\u00f4me\" and in 1985 a \"Certificat d'\u00e9tudes approfondies\" (Masters) in wooden building from the same university. In 1983 she attended a seminar called \"Energy planning and the environment\" at the Summer University of Oslo. Since her marriage to a German engineer (1986), she lives in Stuttgart, Germany.\n Dominique Gauzin-M\u00fcller worked as an architect for a few years, before devoting herself primarily to writing and teaching. She lectured at the University of Stuttgart between 1988 and 1989. \n She published Le Bois dans la Construction in 1990. Between 1998 and 2004, Gauzin-M\u00fcller was the editor of the book Jean Prouv\u00e9 - Complete Works, in three volumes, by Peter Sulzer. Since 1994 she has organized over 40 study tours for French architects and engineers in Germany and in the Vorarlberg, including the French National Order of Architects and the National Committee for Development the promotion of Wood (CNDB). In 1995, she received the Prix Henri Courbot for her book Le Bois dans la construction. In 1997 she published, in German, Behnisch & Partner 50 Jahre Architektur on the German architect G\u00fcnter Behnisch, a book which received the prize awarded to the \"50 most beautiful books of Germany\". In 1999, Gauzin-M\u00fcller published Construire avec le Bois, a work for which she received the Prix Henri Le M\u00eame awarded by the French Acad\u00e9mie d'architecture. The same year, she participated in the competition for the Mirecourt High School, Stuttgart, with the architectural firm Jockers. Her most successful book, L'Architecture \u00c9cologique, was published in 2001 and has since been translated into six languages, including Chinese. Gauzin-M\u00fcller participated as co-designer in the project of the Waste-Recovery Center of the \"Parc d'activit\u00e9s\" in Mayenne, led by Joel Gimbert.\n In 2003 she published 25 Maisons en Bois, translated into three languages, and in 2005 25 Maisons \u00c9cologiques translated into five languages. In 2007, the  French Acad\u00e9mie d'architecture awarded her the Prix Dejean \"for studies and research on the implementation of the principles of sustainable development to architecture and urbanism\".[1] In addition to her personal works, Gauzin-M\u00fcller has published essays in numerous books, including \"Une Terre humaine\" in L'Architecte e(s)t l'autre, \"D\u00e9veloppement durable dans l\u2019architecture et l\u2019urbanisme\" and  \"Les \u00e9nergies renouvelables dans le b\u00e2timent\" in the Neufert (2007),  \"L\u2019exemple du Vorarlberg\" in La maison individuelle, \"L\u2019architecture \u00e9co-responsable\" in La science au pr\u00e9sent 2008, une ann\u00e9e d\u2019actualit\u00e9 scientifique et technique of the Encyclop\u00e6dia Universalis.\n She works with several publishers, and has also published essays in community works such as \"Les \u00e9l\u00e9ments des projets de construction\" (Neufert, 2007) or the \"Encyclopedia Universalis\" (2008). Since the early 1980s, Gauzin-M\u00fcller has written for many European architectural magazines: D'Architectures (France), Techniques et architecture (France), Maisons \u00e0 vivre (France), S\u00e9quence bois (France), Architecture int\u00e9rieure cr\u00e9\u00e9 (France), Le Moniteur des travaux publics et du b\u00e2timent (France), L'Architettura naturale (Italy), Deutsche Bauzeitung (Germany), Detail (Germany), etc. \n Gauzin-M\u00fcller, who gives talks all over Europe, was invited to several architectural universities, for example in Vienna, and La Coru\u00f1a (Spain). From 2004 to 2007, she taught at the \u00c9cole Nationale Sup\u00e9rieure d'Architecture in Nancy.
