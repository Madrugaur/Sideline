{"name": "Marta Sanz-Sol\u00e9", "content": "Marta Sanz-Sol\u00e9  (born 19 January 1952 in Sabadell, Barcelona) is a Catalan mathematician specializing in probability theory. \nShe obtained her PhD in 1978 from the University of Barcelona under the supervision of David Nualart.[1]\n Sanz-Sol\u00e9 is professor at the University of Barcelona, and head of the research group on stochastic processes. Prior to taking up her post at the UB, she was associate professor at the University Aut\u00f2noma of Barcelona. She was Dean of the Faculty of Mathematics UB from 1993-1996, and Vice-president of the Division of Experimental Sciences and Mathematics from 2000-2003. In May 2015 she was appointed chair of the scientific Committee of the Graduate School of Mathematics (BGSMath).[2] and from May 2018 until October 2019, she held the position of Director.\n Her research interests are in stochastic analysis, in particular stochastic differential and partial differential equations. She has published more than 90 scientific articles \n[3]\n[4]\nand a monograph on Malliavin calculus and applications to SPDEs.[5]\n Sanz-Sol\u00e9 served in the Executive Committee of the European Mathematical Society in 1997-2004. She was elected President in 2010,[6] and held the post from January 2011 to December 2014.[7] She is a member of several international committees overseeing the mathematical sciences. In particular, the Board of Directors of the Institut Henri Poincar\u00e9, the FSMP (Fondation Sciences Math\u00e9matiques de Paris), the committee for research and education of the \u00c9cole Polytechnique, and the Scientific Committee of CIRM (Centre des Rencontres Math\u00e9matiques, Luminy, France). In the past years, she served in the Scientific Council of the  Banach Center [8] (2010-2014), the Fellows Committee of the Institute of Mathematical Statistics (2012-2014), and the Committee of Special Lectures (2008-2010).[9] She was a member of the Board of Directors of the Centre de Recerca Matem\u00e0tica (Bellaterra, Barcelona) for a three-year period, starting April 2007.[10]  In June 2015, she was appointed member of the Abel Committee for the Abel Prize 2016, 2017.[11]\n In 1998 she was awarded with Narcis Monturiol Medal of Scientific and Technological Excellence [12] by the  Generalitat of Catalonia. In 2011 she was elected Fellow of the Institute of Mathematical Statistics.[13][14]  In november 2016, she was elected numerary member of the Institute of Catalan Studies. [15].In 2017 she was awarded the Real Sociedad Matem\u00e1tica Espa\u00f1ola Medal [16] for her scientific contributions and relevant international positions and service.
