Bickel (1852 \u2013 1 February 1921) was a prominent architect practicing in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.[1]\n Bickel was born to a well-to-do family of Columbus, Ohio who sent him to Europe for six years to prepare him for a career in architecture. On his return in 1875, he settled in Pittsburgh, apprenticed with an architect there. In 1885 he opened his independent practice, at first in partnership with J.P. Brennan, a partnership that was soon dissolved. Bickel's practice at its height averaged $3,000,000 a year in billings and was concentrated in commercial structures. He served for a time as architect to the city of Pittsburgh, and designed and built numerous police precinct houses and the Public Safety offices.\n Failing health forced him to retire in 1920, and he turned his practice over to his son.[2]\n Many of Bickel's commercial structure were of fireproof construction.[3]\n Granite Building (former German National Bank) (1890), in Downtown Pittsburgh.
