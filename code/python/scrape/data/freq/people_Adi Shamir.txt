He is a co-inventor of the Rivest\u2013Shamir\u2013Adleman (RSA) algorithm (along with Ron Rivest and Len Adleman), a co-inventor of the Feige\u2013Fiat\u2013Shamir identification scheme (along with Uriel Feige and Amos Fiat), one of the inventors of differential cryptanalysis and has made numerous contributions to the fields of cryptography and computer science.[3]\n\nContents\n\n1 Education\n2 Career and research\n\n2.1 Awards and honors\n\n\n3 References\n\n\nEducation[edit]\nBorn in Tel Aviv, Shamir received a Bachelor of Science (BSc) degree in mathematics from Tel Aviv University in 1973 and obtained his Master of Science (MSc) and Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degrees in Computer Science from the Weizmann Institute in 1975 and 1977 respectively.[2]\n\nCareer and research[edit]\nAfter a year as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Warwick, he did research at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) from 1977\u20131980 before returning to be a member of the faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science at the Weizmann Institute. Starting from 2006, he is also an invited professor at \u00c9cole Normale Sup\u00e9rieure in Paris.\nIn addition to RSA, Shamir's other numerous inventions and contributions to cryptography include the Shamir secret sharing scheme, the breaking of the Merkle-Hellman knapsack cryptosystem, visual cryptography, and the TWIRL and TWINKLE factoring devices. Together with Eli Biham, he discovered differential cryptanalysis in the late 1980s, a general method for attacking block ciphers. It later emerged that differential cryptanalysis was already known \u2014 and kept a secret \u2014 by both IBM[4] and the National Security Agency (NSA).[5]\nShamir has also made contributions to computer science outside of cryptography, such as finding the first linear time algorithm for 2-satisfiability[6] and showing the equivalence of the complexity classes PSPACE and IP.\n\nAwards and honors[edit]\nShamir has received a number of awards, including the following:\n\nthe 2002 ACM Turing Award, together with Rivest and Adleman, in recognition of his contributions to cryptography[7]\nthe Paris Kanellakis Theory and Practice Award;[8]\nthe Erd\u0151s Prize of the Israel Mathematical Society,\nthe 1986 IEEE W.R.G. Baker Award[9]\nthe UAP Scientific Prize[clarification needed]\nThe Vatican's PIUS XI Gold Medal[10]\nthe 2000 IEEE Koji Kobayashi Computers and Communications Award[11]\nthe Israel Prize, in 2008, for computer sciences.[12][13]\nan honorary DMath (Doctor of Mathematics) degree from the University of Waterloo[14]\n2017 (33rd) Japan Prize in the field of Electronics, Information and Communication for his contribution to information security through pioneering research on cryptography [15]\nhe was elected a Foreign Member of the Royal Society (ForMemRS) in 2018 for substantial contribution to the improvement of natural knowledge.[1]\nHe was elected a Member of the American Philosophical Society in 2019.\n\nReferences[edit]\n\n\n^ a b Anon (2018). \"Adi Shamir ForMemRS\". royalsociety.org.
