Grave of Mara Neusel in Berlin\nMara Dicle Neusel (May 14, 1964 \u2013 September 5, 2014) was a mathematician, author, teacher and an advocate for women in mathematics. The focus of her mathematical work was on invariant theory, which can be briefly described as the study of group actions and their fixed points.\n\nLife and education[edit]\nMara Neusel was born in Stuttgart, Germany, one of two children of G\u00fcnter and Ayl\u00e2 (Helvacioglu) Neusel. In 2001 she became the fourth woman to earn the advanced degree venia legendi (Habilitation) in mathematics from the University of G\u00f6ttingen, following in the footsteps of the first woman mathematician to be awarded the venia legend from G\u00f6ttingen in 1919, Emmy Noether.[1][2]\n\nCareer[edit]\nProfessor Neusel was the author of a research monograph, an advanced undergraduate text, and a memoir for the American Mathematical Society: Invariant Theory and Finite Groups,[3]Invariant Theory,[4] and Inverse Invariant Theory and Steenrod Operations.[5]    The exposition in the text Invariant Theory \"stands out by its masterly clarity, comprehensiveness, profundity, and didactical disposition.\"[6] Neusel served on the editorial boards of Advances in Pure Mathematics and the International Journal of Mathematics and Applied Statistics.  A tireless advocate for girls and women in mathematics, Neusel established  Emmy Noether High School Mathematics Days in May 2003[7] which continues to be celebrated with workshops and mathematical competitions.[8]  She co-founded the Young Women in Mathematics group at Texas Tech and received a diversity grant to support the group.\nNeusel began her career at  Texas Tech University in 2002 as an associate professor and was promoted to full professor in 2009.[9] She also held visiting appointments at Yale University, the University of Minnesota, and the University of Notre Dame.\nProfessor Neusel was had long term memberships in the American Mathematical Society [10] and the Association for Women in Mathematics.[1] Neusel co-organized a special session on \"Homological Algebra and Its Applications\" at the 2005 Meeting of the American Mathematical Society in Lubbock, Texas[11] as well as a special session on \"Commutative Algebra and Algebraic Geometry\" at the 40th Anniversary Celebration of the Association for Women in Mathematics at Brown University in 2011.[12]\n\nReferences[edit]\n\n\n^ a b Marie Vitulli, In Memoriam: Mara Dicle Neusel AWM Newsletter, November\u2013December 2014, vol. 44 no. 6, p. 21: https://www.dropbox.com/s/dcwn31w7v2q63lf/AWM%20News%20Nov%20Dec%202014.pdf?dl=0\n\n^ Bessenrodt, Christine. \"Habilitationen von Frauen\". dmv.mathematik.de/index.php/all-docman-categories/stellungnahmen/stellungnahmen-der-dmv/439-sn-tuc-habilitationfrauen-2000/file.
