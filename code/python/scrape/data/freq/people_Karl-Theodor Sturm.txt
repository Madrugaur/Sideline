{"name": "Karl-Theodor Sturm", "content": "Karl-Theodor \"Theo\" Sturm (born 7.November 1960) is a German mathematician working in stochastic analysis.\n After obtaining his Abitur from the Platen-Gymnasium Ansbach in 1980, Sturm began to study Mathematics and Physics at the University of Erlangen-Nuremberg where he graduated in 1986 with the Diploma in Mathematics and the State Examination in Mathematics and Physics. In 1989, he obtained his PhD (with a thesis on \u201ePerturbation of Hunt processes by signed additive functionals\u201c) under the supervision of Heinz Bauer and in 1993 he received his habilitation.[1] Visiting and research positions led him to the universities of Stanford, Zurich, and Bonn as well as to the MPI Leipzig. From 2007 to 2010, he was Managing Director of the Institute for Applied Mathematics. Since 2012, he is Coordinator (Managing Director) of the Cluster of Excellence \"Hausdorff Center for Mathematics\".[3]\n The focus of his research is in Stochastic and Geometric Analysis.[4] He gained particular attention with his work on \u201aAnalysis on local Dirichlet spaces\u2019, 1993-1995, where he introduced geometric concepts for the investigation of stochastic processes and carried over methods from elliptic regularity theory to singular operators on abstract spaces, as well as with his pioneering work on synthetic Ricci bounds for metric measure spaces.
