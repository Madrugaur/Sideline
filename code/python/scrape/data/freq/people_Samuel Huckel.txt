Huckel and Ruth A. Huckel in 1858.[2]  William Huckel\u2014his father\u2014was treasurer of the Baugh and Sons company of Philadelphia, producers and distributors of phosphate fertilizer and agricultural chemicals.[2][3]\n After graduating from Central High School in 1879, a young Huckel spent a few months studying with painter William E. Winner until he entered the office (then at 1018 Arch Street, Philadelphia[4]) of Benjamin D. Price\u2014church architect and purveyor of imitation stained glass\u2014who then took him on as a pupil.[2]\n Huckel appears to have stayed with Price at least until 1881 when he established a partnership with Edward Hazlehurst to form Hazlehurst & Watson.  Huckel was still partner to Hazlehurst\u2014having offices at 502 W. Chelton Ave., Germantown and living at 1211 Walnut Street, Philadelphia\u2014in 1905.[2][5]  During Huckel's 20-year partnership with Hazlehurst, they built many \"notable country houses, churches, clubhouses, office buildings, banks, boathouses, police & fire stations, and bathhouses\".[6]\n After leaving Hazlehurst, Huckel partnered with Frank Rushmore Watson to form Watson & Huckel.
